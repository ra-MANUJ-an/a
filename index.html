In mid-July ‘21, I’ll be joining Google AI as a research scientist in the Language team.

My research focuses on developing machine learning models that can reason about natural languages, for example English. My work primarily focuses on structured models for grounded language understanding, primarily in the context of making machines understand and answer questions against text. I am particularly interested in models that perform complex compositional reasoning and are able to provide an explanation about their predictions.

I completed my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Pennsylvania pursuing research in Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. I was advised by Prof. Dan Roth.

I am fortunate enough to have gotten the opportunity to work with excellent researchers in the field. I work closely with Prof. Sameer Singh, UCI and Matt Gardner, Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence. In the past, I have worked as an intern with Tom Kwiatkowski, Google Research and Mike Lewis, Facebook AI Research.

Before coming to UPenn, I received my B.Tech - M.Tech dual degree from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur; Prof. Harish Karnick was my thesis advisor.
